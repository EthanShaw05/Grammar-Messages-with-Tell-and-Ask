Messages with
Tell & Ask

Formal and informal messages — how to pass information and requests in English.

Prepared by Luis Ricardo Santana

Overview

In English, when we want to pass a message from someone to another person (like a messenger), we use tell or ask — depending on the type of message we are delivering.

The Two Types of Messages

💬

Statement

You say something or give information to someone else.

"I'm going to miss class tonight."
🙏

Request

You ask someone to do something for you.

"Could she take a picture of the board?"

The Golden Rule

Statement

Use TELL
to pass information

⟶
Request

Use ASK
to pass a favor or action

Levels of Formality

TELL — Statements
Level Structure Example
Informal (Please) Tell him (that) + sentence (Please) Tell him I'm going to miss class.
Medium Could you tell him (that) + sentence Could you tell him I'm going to miss class?
Formal Would you tell him (that) + sentence Would you tell him I'm going to miss class?
ASK — Requests
Level Structure Example
Informal (Please) Ask her to + verb (Please) Ask her to take a picture.
Medium Could you ask her to + verb Could you ask her to take a picture?
Formal Would you ask her to + verb Would you ask her to take a picture?

The Key Trick ⚡

The easiest way to remember which word follows tell and ask:

TELL
is followed by
that + full sentence
Tell him that she is sick.
ASK
is followed by
to + infinitive
Ask her to call me.

Extra Examples

Situation A
"I need help with my homework."
Informal Ask him to help me with my homework.
Medium Could you ask him to help me with my homework?
Formal Would you ask him to help me with my homework?
Situation B
"I'll be late to the meeting."
Informal Tell her that she'll be late.
Medium Could you tell her that she'll be late?
Formal Would you tell her that she'll be late?

Summary

🎓 Remember These 3 Rules

1
Is it information? → Use TELL + that + full sentence.
2
Is it a request? → Use ASK + to + infinitive verb.
3
How formal is it? → Please (informal) · Could you (medium) · Would you (formal).
